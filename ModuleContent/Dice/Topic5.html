Rolling Multiple Dice

When rolling two dice, the total number of possible outcomes is 6 × 6 = 36.

Sum Probabilities:

  • Sum of 7: Most likely (6 ways) = 6/36 = 1/6
  • Sum of 2 or 12: Least likely (1 way each) = 1/36
  • Doubles: 6 ways out of 36 = 1/6
  • Sum of 3: 2 ways = 2/36 = 1/18

Example: Rolling two dice and getting a sum of 7 occurs in 6 ways: (1,6), (2,5), (3,4), (4,3), (5,2), (6,1).

Practice:

  • P(sum of 12) = 1/36 (only one way: 6+6)
  • P(doubles) = 6/36 = 1/6
  • P(sum of 2 or 4) = 3/36 = 1/12
