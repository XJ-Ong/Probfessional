Understanding Basic Dice Probability

A standard die has 6 faces, numbered 1 through 6. Each face has an equal probability of appearing when rolled. The probability of any specific outcome is 1/6.

Key Concepts:

  • Single die: Each outcome has probability 1/6
  • Complement rule: P(not A) = 1 - P(A)
  • For example, P(not 5) = 1 - 1/6 = 5/6
  • Even numbers: There are 3 even numbers (2, 4, 6), so P(even) = 3/6 = 1/2

Practice Examples:

  • What is P(rolling a 6)? Answer: 1/6
  • What is P(rolling an even number)? Answer: 1/2
  • What is P(rolling NOT a 5)? Answer: 5/6
