Independent Events

Each roll of a die is independent - previous rolls do NOT affect future outcomes.

Multiplication Rule: For independent events, P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B)

Examples:

  • Rolling two 1s: (1/6) × (1/6) = 1/36
  • Rolling 3 dice, all showing 6: (1/6)³ = 1/216
  • Rolling 3 dice, at least one 6: Use complement rule
  • P(at least one 6) = 1 - P(no 6s) = 1 - (5/6)³ = 1 - 125/216 = 91/216

Complement Rule: When calculating "at least one" probabilities, use P(at least one) = 1 - P(none)

Practice:

  • Roll twice, P(both are 1) = 1/36
  • Roll twice, P(never get 5) = (5/6)² = 25/36
  • Roll 3 times, P(at least one 6) = 91/216
