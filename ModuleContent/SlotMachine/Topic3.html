Using the Complement Rule in Slot Machine Probability

The complement rule is essential for calculating probabilities of "at least one" scenarios, which are more complex than simple multiplication.

The Complement Rule

  • Formula: P(A) = 1 - P(not A)
  • P(not getting cherry) = 1 - P(cherry) = 1 - 1/10 = 9/10
  • Useful for calculating "at least one" scenarios

''At Least One"Calculations

  • P(at least one jackpot in 3 tries) = 1 - P(no jackpot in 3 tries)
  • If P(jackpot) = 1/250, then P(no jackpot) = 249/250
  • P(at least one) = 1 - (249/250)³ ≈ 0.012 = 1.2%

Multiple Attempt Scenarios

  • P(never getting diamond in 10 spins) = (15/16)^10 ≈ 53.7% if P(diamond) = 1/16
  • P(at least one diamond) = 1 - (15/16)^10 ≈ 46.3%
  • More attempts increase the chance of "at least one" success

Application: This rule applies to any independent repeated trials, not just gambling!
