The Multiplication Rule for Independent Events

Slot machines perfectly demonstrate the multiplication rule: when events are independent, the probability of multiple events occurring together is the product of their individual probabilities.

Understanding the Rule

  • Formula: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B) for independent events
  • Each spin is independent - previous results don''t affect future spins
  • Probability of multiple outcomes multiplies, making rare events extremely unlikely

Practical Examples

  • P(three cherries) = (1/10)³ = 1/1000 = 0.1% if P(cherry) = 1/10 per slot
  • P(same symbol on all three slots) = (1/n)³ where n = number of symbols
  • P(jackpot in 3 attempts) ≠ 3 × P(jackpot) - must use complement rule!

Common Misconceptions

  • Gambler''s Fallacy: "I''m due for a win" - FALSE!
  • Hot Streak: Previous wins don''t increase future probability
  • Machine Memory: Machines have no memory of past spins

Key Insight: Independence means past results cannot predict future outcomes!
