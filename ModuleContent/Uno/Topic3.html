Probability of Drawing Action and Wild Cards

UNO''s special cards (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild) have different probabilities that affect gameplay strategy. Understanding these probabilities helps make better decisions.

Action Card Probabilities

  • Skip Cards: 8 total (2 per color) = P(skip) = 8/108 = 2/27 ≈ 7.41%
  • Reverse Cards: 8 total (2 per color) = P(reverse) = 8/108 = 2/27 ≈ 7.41%
  • Draw Two: 8 total (2 per color) = P(draw two) = 8/108 = 2/27 ≈ 7.41%
  • Wild Cards: 8 total (4 Wild, 4 Wild Draw Four) = P(wild) = 8/108 = 2/27 ≈ 7.41%

Combined Probabilities

  • P(any action card) = 24/108 = 2/9 ≈ 22.22%
  • P(action or wild) = (24 + 8)/108 = 32/108 ≈ 29.63%
  • P(number card) = 76/108 ≈ 70.37%

Game Progression Effects

  • As cards are played, probabilities of remaining cards increase
  • If many action cards are played, remaining deck has more number cards
  • Strategic card counting improves probability estimates

Strategy: Track which special cards have been played to better predict remaining deck composition!
