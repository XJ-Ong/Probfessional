Poker Hands: Flushes, Straights, and Full Houses

Flush (all same suit):

  • Ways to choose 5 cards from 13 of same suit: C(13,5)
  • Total ways to choose 5 cards: C(52,5)
  • P(flush) = 4 × C(13,5) / C(52,5) ≈ 1/508

Straight (5 consecutive ranks):

  • 10 possible straights (A-5 through 10-A)
  • Each can be any suit: 4⁵ = 1024 ways
  • P(straight) ≈ 1/254

Full House (3 of one rank, 2 of another):

  • 13 ways to choose rank for triple, 12 ways for pair
  • P(full house) ≈ 1/693

No Pairs: P(no pairs in 5 cards) ≈ 0.504

Practice:

  • P(flush) ≈ 1/508
  • P(straight) ≈ 1/254
  • P(full house) ≈ 1/693
  • P(no pairs) ≈ 0.504
